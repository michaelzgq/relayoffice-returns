# Returns GTM Positioning Pack

最后更新时间：2026-04-06 America/Los_Angeles

## Goal

把当前产品从“做了一个 returns demo”收口成一套可对外使用的销售与定位材料。

## Executive Conclusion

不要对外卖：

- another returns app
- another shopper portal
- another reverse logistics suite

要卖：

**`Returns Exception Control for Multi-Brand 3PLs`**

一句话版本：

`We help multi-brand 3PLs standardize warehouse-side return exceptions with client-specific playbooks, evidence capture, and refund-release controls.`

中文：

`我们帮多品牌 3PL 把仓库侧高风险退货处理标准化：按客户规则做判断、留证据、控放款。`

## What Changed In Positioning

这一版定位和之前最大的不同，不是更乐观，而是更准确：

1. 不再假设市场空白
2. 承认 `Loop + Two Boxes` 是直接重叠竞品
3. 不再把“多品牌 3PL”本身当作唯一性
4. 把优势改成：`更轻、更快、更 3PL-owned、免集成先上手`

## Verified Competitive Facts

以下是已经核实过的事实，适合内部判断，不建议逐字复制到官网。

### Loop + Two Boxes

- `2024-02-05`，Loop 官宣新 reverse logistics 能力，包含与 Two Boxes 的扩展集成；官方描述覆盖 warehouse grading、disposition、flagging。
- `2024-02-13`，Loop 的官方文章明确写到：Two Boxes 的 inspection 结果会把 return 在 Loop 里自动 `flagged for further review before refund or exchange`。
- `2026-02-24` 更新的 Loop 帮助中心文档写明：Two Boxes 与 Loop 是双向集成，支持将 `grading / disposition / notes` 写回 Loop，并触发 `process or flag`。

结论：

**Loop + Two Boxes 已经覆盖了“仓库 inspection + disposition + refund review”这条线。**

### Two Boxes

- Two Boxes 官网明确同时面向 `Brands` 和 `3PLs`。
- 官网直接写了：`Process any return, from any brand, in seconds.`
- 官网直接写了：`Digitize your brands' SOPs`，以及让仓库员工处理 `any and all of your brands`。

结论：

**Two Boxes 不是单品牌工具，它也在讲多品牌和 3PL。**

### AfterShip Returns

- AfterShip 官方帮助中心已支持 returned item grading。
- 字段包括 `grade`、`notes`、`warehouse_operator`、`inspected_at`、`image_urls`。
- 也支持基于规则自动处理、欺诈工作流、手动 review 和图片上传。

结论：

**AfterShip 不是完全不重叠，只是主叙事更偏 merchant returns 平台，而不是 3PL exception-control layer。**

### ReverseLogix

- ReverseLogix 官方材料明确把 `3PLs` 作为核心 use case。
- 官方公开内容强调：3PL 可以把 returns 变成新的收入线，并提到 `10%-30%` 的 revenue uplift 可能性。

结论：

**ReverseLogix 不是不存在，而是更重、更 enterprise、更像完整 reverse logistics suite。**

## What Is Actually Unique

不要再说：

- 只有我们支持多品牌 3PL
- 只有我们支持 refund gate
- 只有我们做 warehouse-side returns

更能站得住的版本是：

### 1. No-Integration Start

`Give the warehouse a URL and start from a browser.`

你现在最强的差异不是功能总量，而是：

- 不要求先做 WMS 项目
- 不要求先上 Shopify/Loop 技术栈
- 不要求安装专门 app
- 不要求长实施周期

### 2. 3PL-Owned Workflow

你是从 3PL 仓库视角出发，而不是从品牌电商后台出发：

- 一个仓
- 多个品牌
- 多套 SOP
- 多种 evidence 标准
- 多种 refund gate 条件

### 3. Exception-First Instead Of Return-First

不要试图解决全部退货。  
先解决：

- wrong item
- empty box
- opened damaged
- missing parts
- serial mismatch
- 需要 hold refund 的 case

也就是：

**不是 general returns workflow，而是 exception-control lane。**

## Discovery Call Script

### Goal

不要推产品。  
目标只有一个：

**确认对方是否真的存在“仓库侧多品牌 exception-control”痛点。**

### Opening

```text
I’m not trying to replace your returns stack.
I’m trying to understand how your warehouse team handles brand-by-brand return exceptions once the item is physically back.
```

### Questions

1. `When a return reaches the warehouse, who decides what happens next?`
2. `How do inspectors know the rule is different for Brand A vs Brand B?`
3. `Where do you store evidence for wrong item, empty box, damage, or missing parts?`
4. `Who is allowed to move a refund forward if evidence is incomplete?`
5. `What kinds of return cases get challenged most often by your clients?`
6. `How do you spot cases that are stuck too long or missing proof?`
7. `If a new brand starts next week, how do you teach the warehouse team that brand’s return SOP?`

### Good Signals

- they mention SOP docs, Slack, spreadsheets, or training as the system
- they mention client-by-client rule differences
- they mention evidence disputes
- they mention hold/release ambiguity
- they mention backlog or SLA blind spots

### Bad Signals

- they mainly want a customer-facing portal
- they only care about labels/exchanges
- they already have a mature warehouse exception system and are happy with it

## 3-Minute Loom Script

### 0:00 - 0:25 Problem

```text
If your warehouse handles returns for multiple brands, the hard part is not the customer portal.
The hard part starts after the item arrives.
Each brand wants different grading rules, different evidence, and different refund thresholds.
```

### 0:25 - 1:05 Playbooks

```text
Here each client gets its own return playbook.
For this electronics brand, we require serial number, packaging photos, and a hold-first refund default.
For this apparel brand, the evidence is lighter and the recommended warehouse action is different.
```

### 1:05 - 1:50 Inspect

```text
Now the warehouse inspector opens a phone browser, picks the client, marks the condition, and the system suggests the warehouse action based on that playbook.
If required evidence is missing, the case cannot move forward cleanly.
```

### 1:50 - 2:30 Queue

```text
Ops can now review only the cases that need attention.
If the evidence is incomplete, the refund stays blocked.
If the case is complete, it can move to ready-to-release.
```

### 2:30 - 3:00 Evidence Pack

```text
Every case has a timeline and export-ready evidence pack.
So instead of arguing from scattered photos and memory, the team has one case record showing what happened and why the refund decision was made.
```
