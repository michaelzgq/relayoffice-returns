## First 10 Account Rule

Do **not** try to represent the entire market in the first batch.

The first `10` should be biased toward accounts that are easiest to learn from:

- multi-brand 3PLs
- smaller or mid-sized operators
- warehouse-heavy teams
- visible operations leadership
- signs of manual SOP-driven exception handling

Do **not** use the first `10` on:

- giant enterprise accounts with long procurement cycles
- brands that only care about shopper portal experience
- companies whose job posts or site language are mostly about shipping labels, exchanges, and portal UX
- teams that clearly want a WMS replacement or deep ERP integration first

## Qualification Gate

An account is good enough for batch one if it matches at least `3` of these:

- serves multiple brands
- is a 3PL or warehouse operator
- has an ops, warehouse, or returns manager you can identify by name
- shows signs of manual process, SOP, QC, inspection, or exception handling
- likely handles damaged, disputed, or high-touch returns
- small enough that a founder-led conversation can still happen quickly

If it matches fewer than `3`, skip it for now.

## Target Roles

Use this priority order:

1. `Owner`
2. `Founder`
3. `COO`
4. `Operations Manager`
5. `Warehouse Operations Manager`
6. `Returns Manager`
7. `Client Success Lead`

Do not start with:

- generic marketing titles
- software engineers
- finance-only contacts
- shopper CX contacts unless they also own warehouse exceptions
